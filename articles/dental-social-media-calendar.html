How to Create a Dental Social Media Content Calendar

By Dental Marketing Society March 5, 2026 8 min read

A content calendar is the difference between dental practices that post sporadically (whenever someone remembers) and practices that maintain consistent, strategic social media presence. A good content calendar ensures you're always prepared, you never miss posting windows, and your team knows exactly what to post when.

This guide walks you through creating and managing a social media content calendar that drives engagement and builds patient relationships.

Why You Need a Content Calendar

Without a calendar:

  • You post randomly, inconsistently, or not at all
  • You waste time deciding what to post
  • You miss optimal posting times
  • Your team doesn't know their social media responsibilities
  • You can't track what's working and what isn't

With a content calendar:

  • Posts go out consistently on a schedule
  • Content is planned strategically (mix of educational, promotional, behind-the-scenes)
  • No more guessing when to post (data-driven timing)
  • Team accountability (everyone knows their tasks)
  • Easy performance tracking (you can see which posts resonated)

Step 1: Choose Your Calendar Tool

Google Sheets/Excel

Simple, free, and shareable with your team. Create a spreadsheet with columns: Date, Platform (Instagram/Facebook/LinkedIn), Post Type, Caption, Image/Video, Hashtags, CTA, Notes.

Specialized tools:

  • Later: Visual calendar interface, auto-scheduling, analytics. $15-99/month
  • Buffer: User-friendly scheduling, team collaboration, analytics. $15-99/month
  • Hootsuite: Multi-platform management, monitoring, detailed analytics. $49+/month
  • Meta Business Suite: Free Facebook/Instagram scheduling directly from Meta
  • Notion: Highly customizable templates, free for basic use

For dental practices just starting, Google Sheets is free and sufficient. As you grow, invest in a dedicated tool for better analytics and scheduling automation.

Step 2: Define Your Posting Schedule

How often? Post 3-5 times per week on Instagram and Facebook. This is frequent enough to stay visible but not so much that you overwhelm followers.

What days/times? Base this on when your audience is active. For dental patients:

  • Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-12pm: Morning coffee breaks, scrolling at work
  • Tuesday-Thursday, 6pm-8pm: Evening scrolling after work
  • Avoid Mondays and Fridays: Lower engagement, people are focused on work/weekend

Create a posting schedule and stick to it. Your audience learns when to expect your content.

Step 3: Plan Your Content Mix

Decide what percentage of your posts fall into each category:

  • Educational content: 30-40% (tips, explanations, dental health facts)
  • Before/afters and results: 20-30% (showcase your work)
  • Team and culture: 15-20% (humanize your practice)
  • Promotional/offers: 10-15% (drive appointments)
  • Patient testimonials: 10-15% (build social proof)

When planning your calendar, ensure this mix. If you're posting 4x per week, that's roughly:

  • Week 1: Education, before/after, team, education
  • Week 2: Testimonial, education, before/after, promotion
  • Week 3: Team, education, before/after, education
  • Week 4: Promotion, testimonial, education, before/after

This balanced mix keeps followers interested while promoting your practice.

Step 4: Batch Create Content

Instead of creating one post at a time, batch create to save time and effort:

Content creation day 1 (2 hours): Video testimonials

  • Record 4-6 patient testimonials (30-60 seconds each)
  • Get consent for posting
  • Save all videos organized by patient name

Content creation day 2 (2 hours): Educational videos

  • Dentist records 4-6 educational videos (brushing technique, cavity prevention, etc.)
  • Quick editing if needed
  • Organized by topic

Content creation day 3 (2 hours): Photography

  • Photograph office, team, before/afters
  • Get natural lighting and professional angles
  • Shoot 20-30 usable images

In 6 hours of work, you've created 60-80 pieces of content—enough for 3-4 months of daily posts.

Step 5: Fill Out Your Calendar

Sample calendar structure:

  • Date: 3/10/2026
  • Platform: Instagram
  • Post Type: Before/After
  • Caption: [Pre-written caption about cosmetic transformation]
  • Visual: Before/after cosmetic dentistry photo
  • Hashtags: #SmileMakeover #CosmeticDentistry #BeforeAndAfter
  • CTA: "DM for your consultation"

Monthly theme approach: Some practices assign themes to months:

  • March: Smile makeovers and cosmetic dentistry
  • April: Gum health month
  • May: Mental health and smile confidence
  • June: Kids and family dentistry
  • July: Summer smile tips
  • August: Back-to-school orthodontics

This approach ties your content to relevant topics and gives you a unifying theme for the month.

Step 6: Use Scheduling Tools

Schedule your posts in advance instead of posting manually:

Benefits of scheduling:

  • Posts go out at optimal times even if you're not working
  • Consistency (posts never get forgotten)
  • Flexibility (create content when inspired, schedule for later)
  • Time savings (batch schedule 4 weeks of posts in one hour)

How to schedule:

Most tools (Later, Buffer, Meta Business Suite) let you upload content, set posting time, and schedule automatically. 30 minutes of work upfront saves hours of manual posting throughout the month.

Step 7: Track What Works

Your calendar should include a column for performance metrics:

  • Engagement (likes, comments, shares)
  • Clicks to website
  • Messages/inquiries generated
  • Notes on performance

Review metrics monthly to identify patterns:

  • Do before/afters get 2x engagement as testimonials?
  • Do Reels significantly outperform static posts?
  • Which hashtags drive most impressions?
  • What times get the most engagement?

Use these insights to optimize next month's calendar. Double down on what works, experiment less with what doesn't.

Creating Your First Calendar: 90-Day Plan

Month 1: Foundation building

  • Post 3x per week (12 posts)
  • Focus on educational content and before/afters
  • Build up follower base

Month 2: Consistency phase

  • Post 4x per week (16 posts)
  • Mix content types strategically
  • Increase engagement by responding to comments

Month 3: Optimization phase

  • Post 5x per week (20 posts)
  • Focus on highest-performing content types
  • Test new content formats (Reels, Stories)
  • Analyze and refine

Team Accountability

Assign clear responsibilities:

  • Content creator: Shoots and edits photos/videos (dentist or staff)
  • Calendar manager: Plans content, fills calendar (office manager or marketing lead)
  • Scheduler: Uploads to scheduling tool and monitors posting (front desk staff)
  • Engagement lead: Responds to comments, DMs, questions within 2 hours (front desk staff)

When everyone knows their role, social media becomes a team responsibility rather than one person's burden.

Common Calendar Mistakes

Too rigid: A calendar should be flexible. If a major news event happens or an urgent promotion comes up, you should be able to adjust.

All promotional: If 50% of your posts are "book now" and "special offer," followers will mute or unfollow you. Balance promotion with value.

No engagement plan: A calendar says what posts to make, but a strategy says how to engage with comments and messages. Include engagement in your plan.

Never reviewed: If you create a calendar and never look back, you can't improve. Monthly reviews of what worked are essential.

Your Next Step

Start with a simple Google Sheets calendar for the next 30 days. Fill in 12-16 posts with a mix of educational, promotional, and behind-the-scenes content. Commit to posting consistently for 30 days and tracking what resonates. After 30 days, you'll have data showing what your patients engage with, and you can build your next calendar with even better targeting.
