How to Get More Google Reviews for Your Dental Practice

By Dental Marketing Society March 5, 2026 9 min read

Google reviews are the new word-of-mouth. When someone searches for a dentist, they see your star rating before they see anything else about your practice. 5-star ratings and numerous positive reviews directly influence whether someone books an appointment with you or your competitor.

Yet most dental practices struggle to generate reviews. Patients are happy but never think to leave a review. Others would leave one but don't know how. Your job is to make leaving a review as easy and natural as possible—then systematically ask every satisfied patient to do it.

This guide shows you proven strategies to generate consistent Google reviews that boost your local search visibility and patient confidence.

Why Google Reviews Matter

Beyond helping prospective patients make decisions, Google reviews directly impact your rankings:

  • More reviews = higher ranking in Google Maps and local search
  • Higher average rating = better visibility
  • Recent reviews = signals your practice is active and engaging
  • Reviews with detailed text (not just 5 stars) count more than star-only reviews
  • Regular review responses show you care about patient feedback

Practices with 50+ reviews rank significantly higher than practices with 10 reviews, even in competitive markets.

The Best Time to Ask for Reviews

Peak moments of satisfaction: Ask when patients are happiest. The best times are:

  • Right after a successful cosmetic treatment ("You look amazing!")
  • When pain goes away ("That toothache is finally gone!")
  • After a positive patient experience (helpful staff, clean office, short wait)
  • When they express gratitude ("Thank you so much for being gentle")

Avoid asking after difficult procedures or if the patient seemed frustrated.

Strategy 1: Direct In-Office Asking

At the front desk checkout: Train your front desk team to naturally ask for reviews:

"We'd love to hear about your experience. Have you had a chance to leave a review on Google? It only takes a minute and really helps other patients find us."

From the dentist directly: Have your dentist mention reviews during or after treatment:

"I'm so glad we got that crown in today. If you're happy with the results, we'd really appreciate a review on Google. It helps us reach more patients like you."

Important: Never offer incentives for reviews. Google explicitly bans paid or incentivized reviews, and getting caught violates their terms of service.

Strategy 2: QR Code in the Office

Print a professional QR code that links directly to your Google review page. Display it prominently in your office:

  • By the checkout desk
  • In the restroom
  • On the thank-you card you give patients after appointments
  • On receipts

Make it frictionless. Patients see the code, scan it with their phone, and they're instantly on your Google review page ready to leave a review.

Strategy 3: Follow-Up Email Requests

Send a follow-up email 1-3 days after the appointment while the experience is fresh:

Subject: "How was your visit?"

Body: "Hi [Name], We hope you're enjoying your fresh smile! We'd love to hear about your experience at [Practice Name]. If you were satisfied with your visit, would you mind taking 60 seconds to leave us a review on Google? It really helps other patients find us. [Direct link to Google review page] Thank you for being our patient! [Practice Name]"

Include a direct link so clicking the link takes them straight to your review page.

Strategy 4: Text Message Requests

Many patients check texts more frequently than email. Send a text 1-2 days after the appointment:

"Hi [Name]! Hope you're loving your results. We'd love a quick Google review to help other patients find us. [link] Thanks!"

Text links can include QR codes that expand into your review link.

Strategy 5: Leverage Your Website

Add a reviews section to your website: Display your top 5-10 Google reviews prominently on your homepage. Include a "Leave a Review" button that links directly to your Google review page.

Create a dedicated reviews page: "Patient Reviews" pages with embedded Google reviews and links to leave reviews perform well.

Use star ratings in your site header: Display your average rating (e.g., "4.9 stars from 47 reviews") in your site navigation. This social proof is powerful and links to reviews when clicked.

Strategy 6: Respond to Every Review (Good and Bad)

Your response rate matters to Google's algorithm. Respond to every review within 24 hours:

Responding to positive reviews:

"Thank you so much for the kind words! We loved having you in our office and are thrilled you're happy with your smile. We look forward to seeing you at your next checkup!"

Responding to negative reviews: Never get defensive. Respond professionally and offer to resolve the issue privately.

"Thank you for your feedback. We're sorry your experience wasn't what you expected. We'd love the opportunity to make it right. Please contact us at [phone number] or [email] so we can discuss how to improve."

Responding shows you care about feedback and makes prospects trust you more.

Strategy 7: Referral Incentives

You can't incentivize reviews, but you CAN incentivize referrals that naturally lead to reviews.

"Refer a friend to [Practice Name]. If they schedule and complete their first appointment, you'll both receive $25 off your next cleaning."

This creates a natural flow: patient refers friend → friend becomes patient → friend leaves review.

Creating Review-Generating Systems

Automate your review requests: Use automation tools like:

  • Podium: Automatically texts patients after appointments asking for reviews ($300+/month)
  • Google My Business email: Manually send Google's built-in review request emails
  • Review generation services: Companies like Reputation.com automate review requests across multiple platforms

Create a routine: Every Friday, email all patients from the past week asking for reviews. Every Monday, send texts. Make it a weekly habit.

Benchmarks and Goals

How many reviews do you need?

  • 0-10 reviews: Minimal ranking impact, but shows some patient engagement
  • 10-25 reviews: Noticeable improvement in local rankings, visible social proof
  • 25-50 reviews: Strong competitive advantage in most markets
  • 50+ reviews: Dominates local search results in most markets

Review generation targets:

  • Small practice (1,000 patients/year): Target 50-75 reviews/year = 1 review per 13-20 patient visits
  • Medium practice (2,000 patients/year): Target 100-150 reviews/year = 1 review per 13-20 patient visits
  • Large practice (3,000+ patients/year): Target 150+ reviews/year = 1 review per 20 patient visits

A healthy review generation rate is 5-10% of patient visits resulting in reviews. This means you're asking systematically and converting reviews from 5-10% of happy patients.

Common Review-Asking Mistakes

Asking at the wrong time: Don't ask new patients or patients who just had uncomfortable procedures. Ask people who are actively smiling.

Offering incentives: Google bans incentivized reviews. Asking someone to leave a good review in exchange for a discount violates Google's terms.

Not making it easy: If patients have to find your Google page themselves, most won't. Provide direct links.

Ignoring negative reviews: Every negative review you don't respond to signals that you don't care. Respond professionally to all.

Having staff ask awkwardly: Train your team on how to ask naturally. It should feel conversational, not forced.

The Long-Term Impact

Reviews compound over time. Every review you get today boosts your rankings for the next year. A practice that generates 10 reviews per month has 120 reviews per year. In 3-5 years, that's 360-600 reviews—enough to dominate most dental markets.

More importantly, reviews convert prospects into patients. Someone researching dentists sees your 4.8 average from 127 reviews vs. a competitor's 4.6 from 23 reviews. You win the psychological battle before they even call.

Start asking today. Train your team this week. Set up your systems this month. In 90 days, you'll have 20-30 new reviews. In a year, you'll have transformed your online reputation.
