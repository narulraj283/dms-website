How Much Should Dentists Spend on Google Ads? Budget Planning Guide

By Dental Marketing Society March 5, 2026 8 min read

One of the first questions dental practice owners ask when considering Google Ads is: "How much should I spend?" The answer isn't as simple as "$500/month is standard." Your optimal budget depends on your practice goals, market competition, average patient value, and growth objectives.

Spend too little and you won't get meaningful results. Spend too much and you might waste money before you optimize your campaigns. This guide helps you calculate the right budget for your dental practice and understand the cost structure of Google Ads.

Understanding PPC Cost Structure

Cost Per Click (CPC)

You don't pay per impression or per day with Google Ads—you pay when someone clicks your ad. CPC varies dramatically based on keyword, location, and competition.

For dental practices:

  • Local keywords ("Dentist in [city]"): $2-8 per click
  • Service keywords ("Root canal," "Dental implants"): $3-12 per click
  • Branded keywords (your practice name): $0.50-2 per click
  • Competitive services (teeth whitening, cosmetic dentistry): $5-15 per click

Highly competitive markets (NYC, LA, SF) see CPCs 30-50% higher than less competitive markets.

Click-Through Rate (CTR)

Not everyone who sees your ad clicks it. Average dental ad CTR ranges from 2-5%. This means:

  • If your ad appears 1,000 times, expect 20-50 clicks
  • If your average CPC is $5, that 1,000 impressions costs $100-250

Conversion Rate

Not every click converts to an appointment. Average conversion rate for dental ads is 5-15%, meaning:

  • 100 clicks = 5-15 appointment requests
  • If your CPC is $5 and conversion rate is 10%, your cost per appointment is $50

Calculating Your Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)

Your cost per acquisition is the most important metric. It tells you whether Google Ads is profitable for your practice.

Formula: CPA = Total Ad Spend / Number of Conversions

Example:

  • Total spend: $2,000
  • Total clicks: 400
  • Total conversions (appointments): 40
  • CPA = $2,000 / 40 = $50 per appointment

If your average new patient is worth $500-1000 in revenue (initial treatment + future visits), a $50 CPA gives you 10-20x ROI. That's excellent.

A CPA under $100 is generally profitable for dental practices. Above $150 and you're likely overspending unless you're targeting high-value services like implants or cosmetic dentistry.

Benchmarking Your Budget Against Your Goals

Scenario 1: Wanting 10 New Patients Per Month

If you want 10 new patient appointments monthly via Google Ads:

  • Assume 10% conversion rate: 10 appointments requires 100 clicks
  • Assume $5 average CPC: 100 clicks = $500
  • Estimated monthly budget: $500-700 (accounting for testing and optimization)

Scenario 2: Wanting 30 New Patients Per Month

  • Assume 10% conversion rate: 30 appointments requires 300 clicks
  • Assume $5 average CPC: 300 clicks = $1,500
  • Estimated monthly budget: $1,500-2,000

Scenario 3: Wanting 50 New Patients Per Month

  • Assume 10% conversion rate: 50 appointments requires 500 clicks
  • Assume $5 average CPC: 500 clicks = $2,500
  • Estimated monthly budget: $2,500-3,500

These are estimates. Your actual numbers depend on your market, ad quality, landing page conversion rate, and how well you optimize.

Cost Benchmarks by Market Size

Small Town/Rural Areas (Population under 100,000)

Lower competition typically means lower CPCs. You might see $2-4 average CPC.

Budget recommendation: $300-800/month to start

Mid-Sized City (100,000-500,000 population)

Moderate competition. CPCs typically $3-6 depending on your specific services.

Budget recommendation: $500-1,500/month to start

Large City (500,000+ population)

Higher competition means higher CPCs. Expect $5-15+ average CPC.

Budget recommendation: $1,000-3,000/month to start

Major Metro (10+ million, NYC/LA/SF)

Extremely competitive. CPCs can be $10-25+. Highly saturated market.

Budget recommendation: $2,000-5,000/month to start

Cost Benchmarks by Service Type

General Dentistry / Cleanings

Lower average patient value. Expect higher volume at lower CPC.

  • Average CPC: $2-5
  • Average CPA: $30-75
  • Recommended monthly spend: $300-1,000

Cosmetic Dentistry (Whitening, Veneers, etc.)

Higher value per patient. Higher CPCs due to competition.

  • Average CPC: $4-10
  • Average CPA: $60-150
  • Recommended monthly spend: $500-2,000

Dental Implants

Highest value service. Higher CPCs but also higher patient lifetime value.

  • Average CPC: $8-15
  • Average CPA: $100-200+
  • Recommended monthly spend: $1,000-3,000

Orthodontics

Long treatment duration means high lifetime value.

  • Average CPC: $5-12
  • Average CPA: $75-150
  • Recommended monthly spend: $500-2,000

Emergency Dental

High intent, often lower CPC competition.

  • Average CPC: $2-6
  • Average CPA: $40-100
  • Recommended monthly spend: $300-1,500

ROI Calculation: Is Google Ads Worth It?

Step 1: Calculate your average new patient value

This is what a new patient is worth to your practice. Include:

  • Initial appointment/consultation fee
  • Initial treatment (cleaning, X-rays, exams)
  • Average lifetime value (how many years will they be a patient? How much do they spend annually?)

Example: A new patient typically completes $300 in initial treatment and returns for maintenance visits for an average of 3 years at $200/year. New patient lifetime value = $300 + ($200 x 3) = $900

Step 2: Calculate your target CPA

Your CPA should be no more than 20-25% of your new patient lifetime value. If your new patient is worth $900, you shouldn't pay more than $180-225 to acquire them.

Better target: 10% of lifetime value. In this example, target CPA of $90.

Step 3: Calculate monthly ad spend needed

If your target CPA is $90 and you want 20 new patients per month:

  • $90 CPA x 20 patients = $1,800 monthly budget

Step 4: Calculate ROI

  • 20 new patients x $900 lifetime value = $18,000 revenue
  • $18,000 revenue / $1,800 ad spend = 10:1 ROI

This is excellent ROI. At 10:1, your Google Ads is profitable and worth scaling.

Budget Allocation Strategy

Don't put all your budget into one campaign type. Diversify across different services and keywords.

Example allocation for $2,000/month budget:

  • Branded search (your practice name): 15% = $300
  • Local search (dentist + city): 35% = $700
  • Service-specific search (teeth whitening, implants, etc.): 35% = $700
  • Local Services Ads: 15% = $300

This diversification reduces risk. If one campaign underperforms, others sustain growth.

When to Increase Your Budget

Increase budget when:

  • Your average CPA is below your target (you're spending less than planned to acquire patients)
  • Your conversion rate is 10%+ (good quality traffic)
  • You're consistently hitting your new patient goals
  • You have positive cash flow from Google Ads patients

Increase gradually. Don't 10x your budget overnight. Try 25-50% increases weekly and monitor performance.

When to Decrease Your Budget

Decrease budget when:

  • Your CPA is consistently above your target and not improving
  • Conversion rate drops below 5%
  • Your practice is at capacity (you can't handle more patients)
  • Your ROI turns negative (spending more than you're making)

Before decreasing, try optimization: improve landing pages, test new ad copy, refine keyword targeting. Only decrease if optimizations don't work.

Budget Timing: Monthly vs Annual

Monthly budgeting: Most dental practices budget monthly. This gives flexibility to adjust based on performance. Seasonal considerations:

  • January-March: Higher demand, increase budget
  • April-July: Moderate demand, maintain budget
  • August-September: Back-to-school drives orthodontics, increase budget
  • October-December: Holiday season, variable (cosmetic procedures up, cleanings down)

Annual budgeting: Some practices commit to annual budgets for consistency. Works if your market is stable year-round.

Common Budget Mistakes

Starting too small: $100/month rarely generates enough clicks to optimize and improve. Start with at least $300-500.

Starting too big: $5,000/month for a practice with no Google Ads experience can lead to wasted spend before optimization. Start conservative, scale on success.

Not tracking ROI: Many dentists don't properly calculate ROI, so they don't know if Google Ads is profitable. Implement conversion tracking and calculate CPA monthly.

Inconsistent budgeting: Starting at $1,000, dropping to $300, jumping to $2,000 prevents optimization. Consistency allows Google's algorithms to learn and improve over time.

Ignoring seasonality: Same budget year-round ignores seasonal demand variations. Adjust budget based on what's happening in your market.

The Bottom Line

There's no one-size-fits-all Google Ads budget for dentists. Start by calculating your new patient lifetime value, determine a target CPA (aim for 10-20% of lifetime value), and build your budget from there. Start with 3-6 months of testing to find your optimal CPA and conversion rates. Then scale confidently knowing your ROI is positive.

A well-optimized Google Ads campaign should deliver new patients at a cost significantly below your patient lifetime value. When it does, scale aggressively.
