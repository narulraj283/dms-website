Patient Reactivation Emails: Win Back Inactive Dental Patients

By Dental Marketing Society March 5, 2026 10 min read

Every dental practice has a graveyard of inactive patients. These are people who came in once or twice, were satisfied with their experience, but then... disappeared. They didn't leave on bad terms. They just stopped coming and went to a different dentist (or didn't go at all).

Reactivating these patients is one of the highest-ROI activities you can do. It costs 5-7x more to acquire a brand new patient than to win back an existing one. Plus, reactivated patients have higher lifetime value—they already trust you and know your practice.

This guide walks you through creating a systematic reactivation campaign that brings these dormant patients back.

Define "Inactive" for Your Practice

Before you can reactivate inactive patients, you need to define what "inactive" means. This varies by practice and patient type.

General guidelines:

  • Regular patients should come every 6 months (cleanings, checkups)
  • If it's been 6+ months since their last appointment, they're becoming inactive
  • If it's been 12+ months, they're definitely inactive
  • For cosmetic/elective patients, 12-18 months before reaching out

Run a report in your practice management system to identify patients who haven't been in 6, 12, and 18+ months. This is your pool of inactive patients to reactivate.

Segment Inactive Patients by Type

Not all inactive patients should get the same message. Segment them for more effective outreach.

Inactive patient types:

  • One-time visitors: Came once, never came back. May have found another dentist, moved away, or just didn't like the experience.
  • Regular patients who stopped: Came consistently for 2-5 years then suddenly stopped. Often due to life changes (moved, changed jobs, insurance changes).
  • Cosmetic procedure patients: Came for teeth whitening, Invisalign, or smile makeover. May not feel they need regular care.
  • Emergency-only patients: Only come when they have pain. Don't value preventive care.

Your message to a one-time visitor should differ from your message to someone who was with you for 5 years. Personalization increases response rates significantly.

Timing: When to Send Reactivation Campaigns

The longer patients have been inactive, the more urgency and personalization your message needs.

Reactivation timeline:

  • 6 months inactive: Send a friendly reminder. "It's been 6 months since we've seen you. Time for your next cleaning?"
  • 12 months inactive: Send a more direct message with urgency. "We miss you! It's been a year. Let's get you back on track with your dental health."
  • 18+ months inactive: Send a win-back offer. Offer a special discount or incentive to motivate them to return.

Send reactivation campaigns systematically. Ideally, every 6 months, identify new inactive patients and launch campaigns to bring them back.

Reactivation Campaign Messaging

Your message matters more than any other element. It needs to be friendly (not guilt-tripping), personalized, and benefit-focused.

Messaging framework:

  • Acknowledge the gap: Don't pretend they were just in last month. "We realized it's been [X time] since we last saw you."
  • Empathize: Life happens. "We know life gets busy."
  • Remind of value: "Regular dental checkups and cleanings catch problems early, save money on expensive treatments, and keep your smile healthy."
  • Make it easy: Specific next step. "Book your appointment in 60 seconds online using the button below."
  • Add incentive: Special offer. "Come back and receive 25% off your first appointment as our welcome back gift."

Example reactivation email template:

"Hi [Patient Name],

We noticed it's been [time] since you last visited us at [Practice Name], and we miss seeing you!

Whether life got busy or you just needed a break, we'd love to have you back. Regular dental checkups prevent costly problems down the road and keep your smile looking and feeling great.

As a welcome back, we're offering you 25% off your next appointment. Just click the button below to book your appointment in 60 seconds. If online scheduling doesn't work for you, give us a call at [phone] and we'll find a time that works.

Looking forward to seeing you soon,

[Dentist Name]"

The Reactivation Email Sequence

A single email rarely reactivates a patient. Use a sequence of emails over time to increase response rates.

3-email reactivation sequence (over 21 days):

Email 1 (Day 1): "We Miss You"

Friendly, no pressure. Acknowledge absence, remind of value, mention special offer. Link to online scheduling.

Email 2 (Day 7): "Special Offer Expiring Soon"

Your offer is expiring. Last chance to take advantage. Shorter, more direct. "Your 25% welcome-back discount expires in 7 days. Book now →"

Email 3 (Day 21): "Last Chance"

Final reminder. Offer is expiring today. "This is your last day to receive 25% off. Book today →" If they still don't respond, mark as "hard inactive" and try again in 6 months.

This sequence typically reactivates 10-15% of inactive patients. If you have 100 inactive patients and reactivate 12, that's 12 appointments—potentially $3,000+ in revenue from a few emails.

Special Offers That Drive Reactivation

Most inactive patients need an incentive to return. But the incentive matters less than the ease of responding.

Effective reactivation offers:

  • Percentage discount: "25% off your first appointment" - Simple and valuable
  • Free add-on: "Free professional whitening with your next cleaning" - Costs you little, appeals to cosmetic-minded patients
  • Package discount: "Buy 3 cleanings, get 1 free" - Encourages future visits too
  • Free service: "Free dental cleaning (cleaning only, exam additional $)" - Easy entry point
  • Low-barrier offer: "Free re-exam and updated X-rays" - Let them re-establish with practice

The best offer is the one that gets them back in. Once they're back, deliver great service and they'll stay.

Multi-Channel Reactivation (Not Just Email)

Email works, but combining email with other channels increases response rates dramatically.

Multi-channel reactivation approach:

  • Email (primary): Send the 3-email sequence above
  • Postcard (secondary): Mail a physical postcard with the same offer. Takes 7-10 days to arrive but breaks through email clutter. "We Miss You, [Patient]. Come Back and Receive 25% Off."
  • Phone call (optional): For VIP patients or major patient losses, a friendly call works. "Hi [Name], this is [Dentist]. I realized we haven't seen you in over a year and I wanted to personally invite you back."
  • SMS/text (if opted-in): "Hi [Name], it's [Practice]. We have an opening on Thursday at 2 PM. Does that work? Reply YES to book."

Email + postcard typically reactivates 25-35% of inactive patients—more than double email alone.

Handling Non-Responders

After your 3-email sequence, some patients still won't respond. Don't give up entirely.

Follow-up for non-responders:

  • Move to lower-frequency list: After 3 reactivation attempts, move them to your monthly newsletter but stop aggressive reactivation attempts
  • Seasonal campaigns: "New Year, New Smile" (January), "Summer Smile Ready?" (May), Holiday promotion (November)
  • Trigger-based campaigns: If they engage with email (open or click), send a follow-up offer. If they continue ignoring, stop emailing temporarily.
  • Annual attempt: Try reactivation once per year with a fresh offer

Some patients truly are gone (moved, found another dentist, unhappy). But 10-15% will eventually respond to the right offer at the right time.

Tracking and Measuring Reactivation Success

You can't improve what you don't measure. Track these metrics for your reactivation campaigns:

  • Email open rate: What percentage opened your reactivation email? (Goal: 20%+)
  • Click rate: What percentage clicked the scheduling link? (Goal: 3%+)
  • Reactivation rate: What percentage actually booked and came in? (Goal: 5-15%)
  • Revenue per campaign: If 100 inactive patients are contacted and 12 rebook at $150 average, that's $1,800 revenue
  • Cost per reactivation: Divide total campaign cost by number reactivated. (Email: $0.50, postcard: $2.00 per patient)

A reactivation campaign that costs $200 (postcard + email) and brings back 12 patients at $150 each = $1,800 revenue, or $1,600 profit. That's an 8x return on investment.

Prevent Patient Attrition (Prevention vs. Reactivation)

The best reactivation campaign is preventing patients from becoming inactive in the first place.

Proactive strategies:

  • Appointment reminders: Email/SMS reminders 48 hours before appointment. People don't forget appointments they're reminded about.
  • Post-appointment follow-up: After cleaning, email: "Hope your cleaning went well! Next appointment is recommended in 6 months. Book now →"
  • Scheduled reminders: If a patient hasn't booked in 5 months (when 6-month appointment is due), send: "Time for your next cleaning! Book today →"
  • Regular newsletter: Monthly emails keep your practice top-of-mind. Patients are less likely to drift to competitors if they hear from you regularly.
  • Loyalty programs: Reward regular patients. "See us every 6 months and earn rewards."

Prevention is easier and cheaper than reactivation, but both are necessary components of a healthy dental practice.

Reactivation Campaign Checklist

  • Run inactive patient report (6+, 12+, 18+ months)
  • Segment inactive patients by type (one-time, regular, cosmetic, emergency-only)
  • Create email sequence (3 emails over 21 days)
  • Choose reactivation offer (discount, free service, package)
  • Set up email automation in your email platform
  • Optional: Design and mail reactivation postcards
  • Launch campaign
  • Track open rates, click rates, reactivation rate
  • Follow up with successful reactivations (ensure they stay active)
  • Repeat quarterly with new inactive patient cohorts

Patient reactivation is one of the most profitable marketing activities you can do. It requires minimal investment and generates significant returns. If you've never run a systematic reactivation campaign, start this month. You'll be surprised how many patients come back with the right message and incentive.
